3) Supplier and Purchase Performance Scorecard
Title: Supplier Scorecard for Local and Imported Purchases.
Description: Build a simulated procurement dataset covering supplier lead time, on-time delivery rate, order accuracy, price variance, delay frequency, and purchase order cycle time. This project connects to local and import purchase process management and supports decision-making with measurable supplier performance.

Problem: Without supplier scorecards, purchasing teams may keep buying from suppliers who are slow, inconsistent, or expensive, which causes hidden cost and service problems. The JD requires analytical skill, attention to detail, and problem solving, all of which this project demonstrates.

Solution: Use Python/Jupyter Notebook to score suppliers, rank them by category, and identify best/worst performers. Then show a simple scorecard for monthly review and a Streamlit dashboard for filtering by supplier country, product family, and procurement type.

Recommendation: Include a recommendation engine such as “retain,” “monitor,” or “replace” supplier based on thresholds. That makes the project feel like a real business tool, not just a report.

Stack to use: Python, Pandas, Jupyter Notebook, Streamlit, Plotly.
